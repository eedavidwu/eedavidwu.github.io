C-ReLIC: Lifting-Inspired Latent Diffusion for Universal Compressed Image Restoration

Di You, Haotian Wu, and Pier Luigi Dragotti
Department of EEE
Imperial College London

Abstract

Compressed image restoration (CIR) aims to recover high-quality images from lossy compressed inputs. While methods based on latent diffusion models (LDMs) achieve strong perceptual restoration under specific settings, extending them to universal CIR remains challenging: task-specific conditional models require costly retraining for new compression settings, and most plug-and-play (PnP) solvers struggle to handle diverse modern codecs. To address these limitations, we propose C-ReLIC (Compressed image Restoration with Latent Invertible Consistency), a unified diffusion-based framework for universal CIR that generalizes across compression settings without explicit codec labels. C-ReLIC builds an LDM-based restoration prior and introduces measurement-consistent latent guidance (MCLG) to guide the reverse diffusion trajectory. MCLG uses a degradation-aware latent lifting transform (D-LiFT) operator to disentangle and recombine measurement- and detail-related information in VAE latent space, with a reference-observation uncertainty map for spatially adaptive fidelity-perception control. Extensive experiments show that C-ReLIC achieves state-of-the-art performance across diverse codecs, and flexibly improves existing LDM-based CIR pipelines as a PnP module.

Overview

Overview of the proposed C-ReLIC framework. (a) The inference pipeline combines a universal diffusion prior (Part I) with PnP measurement-consistent latent guidance (Part II). (b-c) Within MCLG, the forward D-LiFT operator decomposes the predicted latent $\mathbf{z}_0^t$ into an observation component $\mathbf{c}_K^t$ and multi-level details $\mathbf{d}_{1:K}^t$ (illustrated with a 2-level cascade). The inverse path recomposes a measurement-consistent latent $\mathbf{z}_{\mathrm{mc}}^t$ by replacing the observation component with the compressed latent $\mathbf{z}_y$. (d) DAP-P/U enables degradation-aware feature adaptation within D-LiFT. (e) Pre-computed residual maps ($\mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{mc}}$, $\mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{ref}}$) spatially fuse $\mathbf{z}_{\mathrm{mc}}^t$ and $\mathbf{z}_{\mathrm{ref}}$ into the final guidance target $\mathbf{z}_{\mathrm{tar}}^t$.
