Leveraging Overfitting for Low-Complexity and Modality-Agnostic Joint Source-Channel Coding

Haotian Wu, Pier Luigi Dragotti, and Deniz Gündüz
Paper Code

Abstract

Deep joint source-channel coding (DeepJSCC) enhances transmission efficiency by jointly exploiting source and channel statistics using deep neural networks. However, competitive DeepJSCC methods typically rely on advanced architectures, high decoding complexity, and large-scale training dataset, limiting their real-world applicability. This paper proposes Implicit-JSCC, a novel overfitted paradigm that directly optimizes channel symbols and a lightweight neural function for each source sample. By performing instance-specific optimization, it eliminates the need for pre-collected datasets and pre-trained model storage. As a low-complexity alternative to standard DeepJSCC, Implicit-JSCC achieves efficient image transmission with around 1000× lower decoding complexity, requiring only 475 model parameters per sample and 546 multiplications per pixel. This storage-efficient design generalizes well across diverse source distributions and achieves new state-of-the-art results on high-resolution data, highlighting the promise of overfitted codecs for future communication systems, especially in streaming scenarios where one-time encoding supports multiple decodings.

From AE-based codec to overfitted codec

The capacity of the action-state channel without reward constraint is given by:

\[ C = \max_{\{\pi(x|s):x\in \mathcal{X},s\in \mathcal{S}\}} I(X; S^+|S), \]

where \( X, S \), and \( S^+ \) follow a joint distribution given by:

\[ p(x,s^+,s) = \rho_\pi(s)\pi(x|s){T}(s^+|s,x), \quad x \in \mathcal{X}, \ s, s^+ \in \mathcal{S}. \]

Towards ultra low-complexity and modality-agnostic DeepJSCC

The capacity of the action-state channel with reward constraint \(V\) is the optimal value of the following convex optimization problem:

\[ \max_{w \in \mathcal{W}} \ I(w,{T}) \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \sum_{s\in \mathcal{S}}\sum_{x\in \mathcal{X}} w(s,x)r(s,x) \geq V \]

where \(I(w,{T})\) is a concave function of \(w \in \mathcal{W}\) defined as:

\[ I(w,{T}) \triangleq \sum_{s\in \mathcal{S}} \sum_{x\in \mathcal{X}} w(s,x) \sum_{s'\in \mathcal{S}} {T}(s'|s,x) \log \frac{{T}(s'|s,x)\sum_{x''}w(s,x'')}{\sum_{x'} {T}(s'|s,x')w(s,x')}. \]
